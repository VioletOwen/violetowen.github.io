Random Musings

Violet Owen 03/01/2021 0 Comments

Today, I learned to Ravi Zacharias’s sexual misconduct. Oh, how many people fall to it, how many people’s witnesses are marred by it. I struggled and am struggling with sexual sin, and it is hard. I still fall, and I have not been “clean” for more than a few months. I hope that marriage helps me with it, but I do not know. It will only be by God’s grace do I conquer it. But still, the news shakes me, or rather, it is a strong warning. I would hate for someone’s reputation, especially one of Ravi Zacharias’ to be tarnished, but one cannot cover up the truth. He could be one of the greatest modern Christian apologists, but he was not perfect.

I do not know what kind of opinion I hold about him: he is a liar, a framer, and a fraud. He tore apart a well-intentioned couple; he sabotaged someone’s attempt at making him confess and healing herself. He did it all in the name of the Lord, but God is just, righteous, and perfect. He does not lie, He does not cheat, He does not obscure the Truth (I’m the Truth, the Way, and the Life). Ravi Zacharias left a great legacy, but can I still recommend him knowing the sin that he committed? Knowing that he did not embody the Truth that he spoke about? Knowing that he framed others and abused others? Knowing that he justified it with saying that it is just a small indulgence and God will forgive and it’s something that he deserves? All men have their weaknesses, but to have this? Who am I to judge? I struggle with the same sin, but still objectively, God condemns it and it is sinful, dirty, and unpure.

Oh, give me wisdom to discern and to come to an opinion that allows me to keep in mind the great things that God used him to accomplish, and to not brush away his sin.

*name redacted*. What dreams, what desires that name brings to mind. What torments, what thoughts, what castles, what joys, what upsets. And he barely knows. He was my first serious crush, and yet something in me says that he isn’t just a crush, but something more. I don’t know—I’m just waiting.
